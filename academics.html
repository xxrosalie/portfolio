CSC461

CSC486 is the class Computer Graphics. In this class, we coded 5 projects using computer graphics tools and algorithms. Projects 1 through 4 were practicing and learning basic computer graphics coding, such as raytracing, rasterization, shaders, textures, and transformations. Project 5 was to build a Battlezone game with the use of all the techniques we learned in projects 1-4. I made two versions of this game. Version 1 is the regular arcade vector-graphics styled Battlezone game. The second version used OBJ models instead of vector-graphics. I initially created OBJ models with textures, but coding the textures was not possible within the timeframe I had, so right now you can only see the non-textured OBJ models. My initial idea was to make it a candyland theme, so with lollipops as obstacles, pink tanks, and pink snowy mountains. The game is played by you, a tank, and you have to kill the enemy tanks before they kill you. If you get hit, you die, your score goes back to zero, and you respawn in the original location. While alive, you can hit enemy tanks and each kill gets you 250 points. Both you and the enemies cannot hit through obstacles or the mountains. There are always two enemy tanks on the battlefield at a time and they wander for about 6 seconds before turning to you to shoot. I had a lot of fun creating this game and figuring out how I wanted all the game mechanics to work.

  • Tools: JavaScript, WebGL, VSCode, HTML, JSON, BlockBench
  • Skills: Computer Graphics coding, OBJ and JSON models, Raytracing, Rasterization, Textures, Transparency, Shaders, Transformations

GC420

GC420 is the class Visual Thinking. This class was offered to all Computer Science students since visual thinking is an important skill for a computer scientist to have. I personally also loved this class because each homework required drawing, which is one of my hobbies. Each week in this class, we would have to draw something different and use our creativity to make it different than the drawings of other students. Each drawing taught us another visual thinking technique. At the end of the semester, we had to choose one visual thinking technique/topic that we learned about throughout the semester and make a large art piece about it. My final project can be seen here. I chose to make it about the topic "Recentering" which explains that people tend to see every object, person, or concept in a stereotyped way at first, but if you recenter your brain, you can see other versions or aspects of the thing in a more creative way. I tried to show this process with a girl. The stereotyped version of her is drawn on the black paper, then the green "glitches" show the process of recentering how you view this girl which shows that you can also see her in other ways such as having curly hair, freckles, imperfections, etc. Neither view is "correct" but it shows that there are multiple ways of seeing things besides the stereotyped view you have when first thinking of it in your brain.

  • Tools: Sketchbook, Pencil, Pen, Sharpie, Colored pencils, Markers, Paint
  • Skills: Visual Thinking, Drawing, Painting, Creative Thinking

HON314 is the class Shakespeare and Leadership. In this class, we read a few of Shakespeare's stories, such as King Henry IV, King Henry V, Julius Caesar, and King Lear. These stories were read in order to examine the characteristics of leadership within these stories and the importance of leadership within the societies in which these stories were written. Authority, responsibility, and accountability are three characteristics that were discussed to asses leadership. For this class, I wrote an essay on how each story depicts leadership. This essay can be seen here.

  • Tools: Microsoft Word

One of the amazing things about this class was the trip we got to take to Staunton, Virginia. We went there to see a Shakespeare play: Much Ado About Nothing. Seeing this play in person made everything we learned in class even more interesting and it was a lot of fun to get to know my classmates better.

The final project for this class was a game that we made in teams of 4. I was one of the artists for this game and the art for each of the levels that I made can be seen here.
