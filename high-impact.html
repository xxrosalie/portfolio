Study Abroad in Prague

PY123 - Stellar and Galactic Astronomy

The first class I took when I studied abroad in Prague, was Astronomy. Prague was the perfect place to take this class because The Czech Republic has a lot of observatories that we could visit throughout our time there. My favorite project for this class was the final project which was a presentation I made about exoplanets, which you can see here. I loved having the opportunity to take this class since I have always been interested in Astronomy, but it isn't really offered in my major. I learned so much and loved the way my teacher approached this class.

Study Abroad and Internship in Ireland

Internship

In Ireland, I did an internship at a few AI startups at University College Cork. For one of these startups, I was given the task to create an informative video to explain their palliative care product. I made a 1 minute video by scratch using stop motion mechanics and my own digital art. To the right are a few scenes from the video. Besides creating this video, I also had some more technical tasks such as coding data pipelines to transform and merge healthcare data. I also built a Python script that analyzes research papers and generates AI prompts and outcomes. Next to that, I tested multiple AI-driven products and websites. The combination of technical, creative, and professional tasks made this internship so beneficial for my educational and personal growth.

E497 - Engineering Research Projects

One of the classes I took in Ireland was E497, which is Engineering Research Projects. In this class, we were required to write a research paper related to our internships that compares something in Europe and the USA. I wrote about AI regulations in Europe vs the USA because the startups I was working with were in their regulatory phases, so I could interview many coworkers to help with this paper. This topic was particularly interesting for a comparative research paper since the AI regulatory frameworks are so different in the US and Europe.

Quantum Computing Research

For about a year, I worked on a quantum computing research project alongside a professor at NC State University, Dr. Yuan Liu. He hired me to start working on a new research project that he hadn't even started yet, so this was an opportunity for me to see the start of a project. I started by conducting a literature review to understand more about our topic, which was attempting to find a new quantum algorithm to simulate differential equations with quantum speed-up. Quantum computers have the possibility to speed up solving any problem that a classical computer can solve. The goal of this project is to find a quantum algorithm that can speed up solving differential equations.
My tasks consisted of:

  • Solving complex linear algebra and quantum problems
  • Converting RLC dynamics into a Schrodinger Equation
  • Conducting Hamiltonian simulations of the Schrodinger equation and the classical algorithm
  • Comparing the classical and quantum simulations to find speed-up
  • Coding in Qiskit
  • Reporting my findings in a research paper using Overleaf

This project gave me the opportunity to practice doing research, write research papers, communicate professionally, and work with many professionals in the field.
